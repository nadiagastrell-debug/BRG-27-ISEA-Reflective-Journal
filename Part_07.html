What I learned anyway

Even though I could not do the practical part, I learned how DNS works. Basically, DNS is like the internet's phone book. When you type "google.com" into your browser , DNS transtaltes that name into the actual IP address that computers use to find each other.

The different types of DNS records:

  • A record: This points your domain name to an IPv4 address, which is the most common type.
  • AAA Record: Same thing but for IPv6 addresses; the newer, longer format.
  • CNAME: Creates an alias, like making "www.example.com" point to "www.example.com"
  • MX: Tells emails where to go for your domain

What I actually tried

I practiced using the nslookup command to see how DNS works in real scenarios:
